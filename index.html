Brochure Page 1

The Front Cover: Identity & Vision

To attract visitors, your brochure needs a powerful cover. You must decide on:

  • The Name: (e.g., The Gallery of Emre, The Teenage Archives)
  • The Hook: A catchy slogan to make tourists want to enter.
  • The Vision Statement: Write 2-3 sentences using unit vocabulary (e.g., preserve, legacy, heritage) explaining *why* this museum was built.
Brochure Pages 2-4

Inside Pages: Exhibit Highlights

Select three specific items from your life (e.g., a skateboard, a ticket, a hoodie). Include a visual for each, accompanied by a Speculative Description guessing how historians in the year 3000 will interpret it.

‚ö†Ô∏è Grammar Requirement: You must use modals of speculation (must have, might be, can't have) for each item!

Example Text:

"This object produced loud noises. Future historians might think it was a weapon. It must have required great skill to hold, but it can't have been comfortable."

Brochure Pages 5-6

Back Cover: Curator's Philosophy

This is where you show deep critical thinking. Visitors want to know the deeper meaning behind your collection. Write 2-3 detailed paragraphs answering these core questions:

  1. The Ultimate Artifact: If this museum burns down in a thousand years, and archaeologists can only save ONE of your artifacts, which one should it be? What does that single item teach the future about what the youth of the 21st century truly valued?
  2. Perspective & Bias: To what extent does perspective and bias influence whether a single artifact can be considered reliable evidence of the values of 21st-century youth?
  3. The Curator's Responsibility: How does the act of selecting and preserving knowledge shape representation, and what responsibilities come with deciding what future generations will know about us?

B2 Level Grading Rubric

Grammar (20 pts)

Accurate and effective use of Modals of Speculation across the descriptions.

Deductions: [-5/-10] Missing modals. [-2/-5] Basic errors impeding meaning.
Vocabulary (20 pts)

Successfully integrates 7-8 target words appropriately in formal context.

Deductions: [-2/-4/-6] Missing target words. [-2/-5] Unnatural context.
Content & Critical Thinking (30 pts)

Philosophy section directly answers TOK-style questions regarding bias, perspective, and responsibility. Descriptions are highly creative.

Deductions: [-5/-10/-15] Philosophy lacks depth or ignores prompts.
Task Achievement & Format (30 pts)

Reaches ~300 words. All required elements/sketches present. Tri-fold brochure layout is well-organized and beautiful.

Deductions: [-5/-10] Missing sections. [-5/-10] Messy layout. [-5] Under word count.

Final Deliverable

Time to build your museum! Take the blank A4 paper given to you by your teacher and fold it carefully into three equal sections (a tri-fold). Use your drafting guide to transfer your text, layout your sketches, and design the final beautiful brochure for The Museum of You.
